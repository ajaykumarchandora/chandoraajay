    Seven Sanskrit Villages of India




    Sanskrit is a language that has had a profound impact on Indian culture and history. To truly understand the richness of Indian culture and philosophy, one must have a deep appreciation for Sanskrit and its many contributions to the world.Sanskrit, the ancient language of India, is losing its values. This is a fact that cannot be ignored. The decline of Sanskrit is a result of various factors. One of the major reasons is the lack of interest among the younger generation to learn and speak Sanskrit. Sanskrit is being preserved by a group of dedicated individuals. Despite the language being considered a "dead" language, these people believe that it is important to keep the language alive. They argue that Sanskrit is the root of many Indian languages and has a rich cultural history.

    In India, there are several villages that are not only preserving but also promoting Sanskrit. One such village is Mattur in Karnataka. The village has made Sanskrit its official language and all the residents speak it fluently. The village also hosts an annual Sanskrit festival which attracts scholars from all over the country.

    Following are the details of some more such villages :

    1. Mattur, Karnataka

    Mattur, a village in Karnataka, India, known for using Sanskrit for daily communication despite the state's general language being Kannada. The village is also recognized as a center for Sanskrit and Vedic studies. The main work of the people of Mattur village is agriculture. They do Areca nuts and Paddy cultivation. But the village is known for the Sanskrit language, which they use for day-to-day communication and also promote Sanskrit to an international level.

    The village has a majority of Sankethi Brahmin community. This is an ancient Brahmin community. This community migrated to Mattur and Hosahalli villages from Kerala 600 years ago. This Brahmin community speaks a special type of Sanskrit dialect called Sankethi dialect. Sankethi dialect is a combination of Classical Sanskrit, Tamil, and Telugu. This dialect has no separate script. Devanagari script is used for this dialect.

    Geography of village :

    The village is located on the left bank of the Tunga River. It is a square-shaped village with a temple and a paathshala in its center. The village has all modern facilities for its residents.
    It has a twin village on other side of Tunga River.

    Daily Lifestyle of Residents

    People in this community lead a Vedic lifestyle. They read ancient scriptures and communicate in Sanskrit. The language is so prevalent that everything from shop signs to house numbers is written in Sanskrit. It's not just adults who speak it either; children are also fluent in the language.
    Sanskrit is not just reserved for religious practices. Even vegetable vendors and teachers use it in their daily lives. The community takes pride in preserving their cultural heritage through the use of this ancient language.
    Living a Vedic lifestyle is not just about language, but also encompasses a way of life that values simplicity and spirituality. This community serves as a reminder of the importance of preserving cultural traditions in a rapidly changing world.

    History.

    The Sankethi Brahmin community had stopped speaking Sanskrit for a long time. In 1981, a non-profit organization called Sanskrit Bharti, based in New Delhi, organized a 10-day workshop to promote Sanskrit. The workshop was successful and on the last day, a ceremony was held with the Prophet of Udupi Pejawar Mutt as the chief guest. Pejawar Mutt said "A place where individuals speak sanskrit, where whole houses talk in sanskrit! what next? A Sanskrit Village."
    The words of the prophet were taken seriously by the village residents. They began promoting Sanskrit in the village, resulting in the creation of a whole Sanskrit speaking community. The children learned Sanskrit from the young generation who were already proficient in the language. Eventually, even the older members of the village learned Sanskrit.

    Process of teaching to children of village

    In Mattur, basic level Sanskrit is taught to students from the age of 10 years. It is a compulsory 5-year course for all students, covering basic shlokas to advance all four Vedas.
    Students not only learn but also expand the script of palm leaves on computers and rewrite the damaged script.

    Learning Sanskrit in Mattur Village for others

    The village attracts students from all over India and the world to learn Sanskrit. There is a Sanskrit Paathshala in the village, which provides different courses to learners. The courses cost at least 10k rupees and include study and accommodation. There are no hotels in the village, but villagers provide facilities for Sanskrit learners for accommodation.

    How to reach

    To reach Mattur, one can reach the state capital city Bangalore by train or flight and then take a bus to Shivmogga town. From Shivmogga, many taxis and buses are available for Mattur.
